# Appendix A: LLM Prompt History & Interaction Logs

## Overview

This repository contains the prompt history and interaction records used during the completion of the AIS5104 Integrative Reflection Essay. The purpose of this appendix is to provide a transparent and reproducible view of how Large Language Models (LLMs) were utilized throughout the writing process.

The full interaction logs have been exported and compiled into a PDF document, which captures the step-by-step collaboration between the user and the LLMs.

---

## LLMs Used

- GPT-5.3 (ChatGPT)
- Gemini 3.1 Pro

These models were used for structured idea generation, iterative refinement, and academic writing support.

---

## Contents

- `prompt_history.pdf`  
  Contains the full sequence of prompts and corresponding outputs, including:
  - Initial brainstorming prompts
  - Outline generation
  - Draft writing and refinement
  - Constraint-based revisions (e.g., word limits, academic tone)
  - Final validation and polishing steps

---

## Workflow Description

The interaction process followed an iterative and controlled prompting strategy:

1. **Idea Generation**  
   Initial prompts were used to generate high-level outlines based on seminar topics.

2. **Draft Development**  
   The outlines were expanded into full responses with academic structure and coherence.

3. **Constraint Refinement**  
   Prompts were adjusted to ensure alignment with assignment requirements, including:
   - Word limits
   - Seminar-specific content
   - Formal academic tone

4. **Critical Revision**  
   Outputs were refined to improve logical flow, remove redundancy, and enhance clarity.

5. **Final Validation**  
   Responses were reviewed and edited to ensure consistency, factual grounding, and alignment with the intended arguments.

---

## Notes

- The LLMs were used as supportive tools for structuring and refining ideas, not as primary sources of factual content.
- All final responses were manually reviewed and validated to ensure accuracy and relevance.
- The prompt history reflects an iterative human-AI collaboration process rather than one-time generation.
